Map & Discovery

Find events near you

The interactive map shows events near you or in any city of your choice. Each colored dot represents an event category. Click a dot to see the event details.
Use the search bar at the top of the map and type a city name. The map automatically zooms to that area and shows available events.
Use the filter panel to select one or more event types: concerts, expositions, markets, sports, conferences and more.
The date filter lets you select: Today, This weekend, This week, or a custom date range. Events outside the range are hidden from the map.
Quick filters are pre-configured shortcuts. Click one to instantly activate a location + period combination. The "July 2026" filter covers the Wikimania period (July 18–27), a major global event.
Open the event detail page and scroll to the Comments section. The discussion thread supports two levels: main comments and direct replies.
Below each comment, emoji buttons are available: 👍 ❤️ 😂 😮. The counter updates in real time for all page visitors.
On the event detail page, you'll find share buttons for WhatsApp, Facebook and X (Twitter), as well as a copy link button.
Open the ☰ menu and click Enable notifications. Your browser will ask for permission — accept to receive event alerts.
On the detail page, click the ⚠️ Report button. Choose the reason (incorrect date, wrong location, duplicate, etc.). Our team handles reports within 24 hours.
The Featured section is visible from the app home screen. It displays as a carousel on mobile and a grid on desktop, with events highlighted by our editorial team.

For organizers

Submit and manage your events

Click the + Add button in the app. Fill in the complete form: title, description, date, location (with address autocomplete), category and access type. Submit — your event will be reviewed within a few hours.
Scan & Publish uses Claude Vision AI to read a photo of your event poster. In about 10 seconds, it automatically extracts the title, date, location, description and category, then pre-fills the form for you.
Yes. If the poster mentions a recurrence ("every Friday", "monthly"), Scan & Publish detects it and automatically pre-fills the frequency and relevant days in the form.

Check the "All day" box in the form. The start and end time fields disappear automatically.
